Days to Hunt
Weekly Structure
Tuesday / Thursday — Gym (60 min): Cardio first (25–30 min incline treadmill with pack, Zone 2), then strength (30 min, 5 exercises). Achilles protocol after.
Day 4 / Day 5 — Home or Outside (45–60 min): Pure Zone 2 aerobic only. Bleachers with pack when possible. Flat neighborhood walk with heavy pack when not. No strength work.
Saturday — Gym or Outside (90 min): Weighted ruck with maximum elevation change. Once per month: 2–3 hour long effort at Zone 2.
Day 4 / Day 5 flexibility: Do them on any two of Mon / Wed / Fri. Pick whichever days you can protect that week.
Bleachers: Descending under load is excellent downhill prep — eccentric quad loading is exactly what gets destroyed on mountain descents. Prioritize bleachers over flat walks whenever possible.
Injury Flags — Stop & Assess
Right leg (hardware): Any twinge at or near tibial plateau under load — hard stop that exercise.
Right leg outer: Outer lower leg twinge during lateral load — reduce weight immediately.
Left knee: Any swelling after a session — reduce load and depth next session.
Left Achilles: Pain at heel insertion — pull the protocol, rest 4–5 days, restart flat ground only.
Left foot: Plantar fascia pain increasing — extra foot rolling, reduce ruck distance temporarily.
Heart Rate Zones (Measured)
Zone 2 target: 137–148 bpm ← all base work stays here
Surge target: 155–165 bpm for 30 sec only (Phase 2+ gym days)
Above 148 on base work → back off grade or pace
Below 137 → increase grade or pack weight
Zones measured directly — not estimated from age formula
Cardiac drift late in long sessions → drop grade, don't push through
5 Core Strength Exercises
Trap Bar Deadlift: Most hunt-specific lift. Simulates picking up a heavy pack or quartering an elk. Even bilateral loading respects right leg hardware.
Bulgarian Split Squat: Single-leg strength and stability on uneven terrain. Closes the bilateral strength gap between legs. Left leg shallow depth only — never chase range of motion.
Step-Up — Broken Leg Lead: Rebuilds right leg motor pattern and neuromuscular confidence. Directly simulates the climbing motion. Zero twinge rule.
Loaded Carry (Farmer or Pack): Trains the trunk stability, grip, and shoulder endurance that keeps you moving under 50 lbs hour after hour. Most underrated exercise in the program.
Pull-Ups: Upper back and lat strength for pack stability, rifle carry, and hauling yourself up steep pitches. Band-assisted to start — goal is 5–10 unassisted by October.
Strength Progression Rules
Add weight when all sets/reps complete and last 2 reps don't feel hard. Never add weight and reps at the same time.
Broken leg (right): Governs step-up and split squat — don't force it. Twinge at tibial plateau = hard stop. Twinge in outer lower leg = reduce load.
Left knee: Shallow depth only on all knee flexion. Watch for swelling after sessions.
Loaded carry: Bilateral farmer carry only until right peroneal strength solid. Suitcase carry reintroduced at 20–25 lbs / 20 yards in Phase 2 only if symptom free.
Rest Protocol (Strength Block)
Trap bar deadlift: 90–120 sec
Split squat / Step-up: 90 sec
Loaded carry: 60–90 sec
Pull-ups: 90 sec
Superset to save time: Step-up + Pull-ups (non-competing — lower body + upper body)
Pull-Up Progression
Phase 1: Band-assisted 3×6–8. Progress to thinner bands until 3×3 unassisted possible.
Phase 2: Max unassisted reps first, finish with band. Add 1 unassisted rep per week.
Phase 3 Peak: Target 3×5–8 unassisted. Add load via vest/belt if reached, reset to 3×3.
Phase 3 Taper: Bodyweight only. 3×5. Maintain, don't push.
Honest expectation: 5–10 unassisted pull-ups by October.
Achilles & Plantar Fascia Protocol
When: After every Tuesday and Thursday session. All 6 months. Non-negotiable.
Foot roll: Lacrosse or golf ball, 2 min each foot, arch and heel. Do this first.
Straight-leg heel raise (flat ground): 3×15 · 2-sec up / 3-sec hold / 3-sec down to neutral. STOP AT NEUTRAL. No step. No below-neutral. Ever.
Bent-knee heel raise (flat ground): 3×15 · Same tempo. Targets soleus — likely culprit from last year's hunt.
No added weight. The controlled tempo is the load.
Ruck & Altitude Notes
Zone 2 aerobic base is the best altitude prep available. Arrive in Colorado 1–2 days early. HR will run higher at altitude — trust feel over zones the first day or two.
Break in hunting boots during ruck sessions starting Month 3. Monitor left Achilles and plantar fascia for any response to boot heel drop change. Ruck in the same pack you'll hunt in.
Downhill prep: Bleacher descents under load are your best tool at sea level on flat terrain. The more sessions the better.
Before Month 2 Begins
Orthopedist: Tell him you're doing progressive loaded carries and rucking to 50 lbs with hardware in the right tibial plateau. Ask what to watch for.
Sports medicine: Full picture — tibial plateau hardware, unaddressed left meniscus, insertional Achilles history, plantar fascia twinges, and this program. One visit gives you a confident foundation.
